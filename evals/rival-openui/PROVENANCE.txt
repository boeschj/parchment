SUPPORTING CHANGES — needed to make their benchmark run at all
================================================================================
These are packaging/import fixes. They change no benchmark semantics and are applied
identically to BOTH runs, so they cannot affect the comparison.

(1) benchmarks/ is not installable as documented.
    Their README says `pnpm install && pnpm bench` inside benchmarks/. That fails:
    benchmarks/package.json declares `"@openuidev/react-lang": "workspace:*"` and
    `"@openuidev/react-ui": "workspace:*"`, but pnpm-workspace.yaml's globs
    (packages/*, examples/**/*, docs/) do not match benchmarks/. So pnpm either
    ignores the package (leaving no node_modules -> "sh: tsx: command not found")
    or, with --ignore-workspace, dies with:
        ERR_PNPM_WORKSPACE_PKG_NOT_FOUND  "@openuidev/react-lang@workspace:*" is in
        the dependencies but no package named "@openuidev/react-lang" is present in
        the workspace

    FIX (pnpm-workspace.yaml):
        packages:
      +   - "benchmarks"
          - "packages/*"

(2) Their sample generator's parser import is dead.
    benchmarks/generate-samples.ts:4 imports:
        ../packages/react-lang/src/parser/parser.js
    That path does not exist at this commit. PR #347 ("Add @openuidev/svelte-lang and
    extract @openuidev/lang-core") moved the parser to packages/lang-core/src/parser/,
    and generate-samples.ts was never updated. `pnpm generate` therefore cannot run.

    We do not call OpenAI, so we do not run generate-samples.ts. Our regen-samples.ts
    performs only its post-model half — parse the committed *.oui, project to the other
    three arms — importing the SAME parser from its current location:
        ../packages/lang-core/src/parser/parser.js

(3) Why we regenerate the derived arms instead of tokenizing the committed files.
    The committed samples were reformatted by `prettier --write .`
    (benchmarks/package.json "format:fix") after they were generated. Prettier collapses
    short arrays onto one line; raw JSON.stringify(x, null, 2) does not. The committed
    *.c1.json files are therefore ~3% SMALLER than what the converter actually emits,
    and `pnpm bench` on a pristine checkout prints -50.3%, not the published -51.7%.

    Regenerating the three derived arms from the committed *.oui with their own
    converters reproduces the published table exactly, in every cell of every column.
    The *.oui files — the only model-generated artifact — are never touched.

    CROSS-CHECK: minifying the committed *.c1.json files by a pure JSON round-trip
    (a whitespace-only transform, no regeneration, no parser) yields token-for-token
    IDENTICAL minified totals (5,461). The two independent paths agree, which proves
    the minify is a pure whitespace change and that the committed samples are
    semantically identical to their current converter's output.
